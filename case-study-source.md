# AI Workflow Case Study: Building a Human-Reviewed Operating System

Relevant for: AI operations, applied AI, AI enablement, deployment strategy, product operations, regulated workflow roles

## Context

I built the operating infrastructure for a source-disciplined AI workflow in a regulated education context. The goal was to turn a changing, high-trust problem into repeatable work: research, source checking, document preparation, conversation prep, follow-up, and delivery.

## What I Built

- A structured dashboard for proposition, segments, product assets, source coverage, and next actions.
- A source register and claims register so guidance stayed traceable to referenced material.
- AI-assisted research and drafting workflows with human review checkpoints.
- Monitoring workflows across education, data protection, exam, safeguarding-related, and government guidance sources.
- CRM and outreach workflows using Attio, Apollo, Google Workspace, Formspree, Netlify, and structured follow-up routines.
- A call console for discovery, qualification, objection handling, and conversation preparation.
- A close-page and follow-up workflow to move from interest to action without losing context.

## Operating Principles

- AI speeds up research, drafting, comparison, extraction, and workflow design.
- Human judgement stays explicit where accuracy, claims, trust, or external communication matter.
- Outputs are useful only when attached to a real workflow, owner, next action, or decision surface.
- The system is designed for iteration rather than a one-time document push.

## Why It Matters

Many teams now need this kind of internal work: taking scattered AI experiments and turning them into working business processes. The evidence here is workflow judgement, implementation discipline, documentation, adoption support, and practical systems thinking.

## What It Shows

- I can structure an ambiguous domain into usable operating workflows.
- I can use AI natively without removing human review.
- I can build dashboards, documents, CRM routines, and decision surfaces around a commercial workflow.
- I understand how to make AI adoption practical for non-technical users.
- I can move from idea to usable system without waiting for a large platform build.
